FAQ

Expand All
  • If my deployed instance is in the “stopped” state, am I still billed?

    Yes. A stopped instance still occupies the computing resources that provide support for the instance, so you will still be charged. In order to stop billing, you need to delete your deployment.

  • If my web or worker role was deployed for less than an hour, how much will I be charged?

    Cloud Services are billed based on the length of time your service was deployed for. If your instance was deployed for less than 1 hour, or several hours plus some minutes that do not add up to 1 hour, you will be charged only for that duration. Azure does not round up partial hours to full hours for billing – you only need to pay for the actual time that your Cloud Services instance was in a “running” state.

  • How are different instance sizes billed?

    As described in the table below, all standard Cloud Services hours are converted to small (A1) instance hours on the bills shown to you. For example, a one-hour medium (A2) Cloud Services instance will appear as a two-hour small (A1) Cloud Services instance on the bill that is shown to you.

    Cloud Services instance Hours Small instance hours
    Extra small (A0) 1 1/4 hour
    Small (A1) 1 1 hour
    Medium (A2) 1 2 hours
    Large (A3) 1 4 hours
    Extra large (A4) 1 8 hours
  • Do the above prices include tax?

    Yes, the prices listed include tax
